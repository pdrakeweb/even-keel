2. Enclosure Interior — Feather-sized IoT Box

Simplified layout: Feather stacked with Featherwing Proto and dAISy, Qwiic chain exits via grommet. No DIN rail, no M12 bulkheads, no power components.

Compared to v1.1 (Polycase WC-23F + DIN rail + 4-6 M12 bulkheads): half the size, no DIN rail, no M12 crimping, no DIY AC-opto wiring, 0 power components inside. Everything vulnerable-to-voltage lives outside.

3. Enclosure Exterior — Simple Front Face

Only four external ports in v1. Phase 8 adds SMA for GPS. Everything plug-in.

Four cutouts in v1 (USB-C, BNC, 2-pin waterproof, grommet) plus three pass-through LED holes. Off-the-shelf boxes often have the USB-C opening already; panel-mount BNC uses a step drill to open one side.

4. Adafruit ESP32-S3 Feather — Pinout Reference Card

Pin assignments for BoatMon-1. Most sensors ride I²C via the on-board STEMMA QT connector — no GPIO allocation needed for them.

board: adafruit_feather_esp32s3 · Featherwing-compatible

5. Power Chain — Externalized USB-C

Radically simpler than v1.1. The boat-node enclosure has one USB-C input. Surge protection and buck regulation live outside in commodity marine hardware.

Everything risky lives outside. No fuse, TVS, bulk cap, or buck inside the boat-node enclosure. Scanstrut handles all of it — and if it fails, it's a 10-minute swap with a screwdriver, not a board-level repair.

6. Sensor Wiring — Qwiic Daisy-Chain

Every I²C sensor connects by plugging in a 50/100/200 mm STEMMA QT cable. No I²C soldering. Only the dAISy UART, DS18B20 bus, bilge float, Tier 0, and starter-V divider require the single Featherwing Proto solder session.

7. Whole-System Topology — Updated

Same three-layer alert model as before, but with Feather + Qwiic in place of DevKitC-1, Shelly plugs in place of opto-isolators, Scanstrut external in place of in-enclosure power components.
